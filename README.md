## 📌 Overview

This repository contains the research paper titled **“A Study on Cybercrime and Security in India”** authored by **Sakshi Paradkar**.  

The study explores the increasing challenges of cybercrime in India, examines cybersecurity practices, and highlights the importance of cyber ethics, data protection, and digital security in today’s digital era.

As India rapidly expands its digital infrastructure through online banking, e-governance initiatives, Aadhaar services, and digital payments, cybersecurity has become a major national concern.

## 🎯 Objectives of the Study

- Identify the major types of cybercrime in India
- Study cybersecurity measures and preventive practices
- Analyze challenges faced by individuals, businesses, and government institutions
- Explore the role of cyber ethics in promoting safe digital practices
- Recommend strategies for strengthening India’s cybersecurity ecosystem

---

## 🛡️ Topics Covered

### Cybersecurity in India
- Digital India initiatives
- Growth of internet users
- Cybersecurity challenges
- National cybersecurity frameworks

### Types of Cybercrime
- Phishing and online scams
- Identity theft and Aadhaar fraud
- Malware and ransomware attacks
- Cyber terrorism
- Cyberbullying and harassment
- Intellectual property crimes

### Major Cyberattack Case Studies
- Cosmos Bank Cyber Heist (2018)
- Aadhaar Data Leak incidents
- WannaCry Ransomware Attack (2017)
- Cyber espionage targeting Indian infrastructure

### Security Practices
- Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
- Firewalls and VPNs
- Encryption methods
- Cyber hygiene awareness
- Policy and legal frameworks

## 📚 Research Methodology

This research follows a descriptive methodology using secondary data sources including:

- CERT-In reports
- NCRB statistics
- Academic journals
- Government publications
- Cybersecurity industry reports

---

## 📈 Key Findings

- Cybercrime incidents in India are increasing rapidly
- Financial fraud and phishing remain dominant cyber threats
- Small businesses remain vulnerable due to limited cybersecurity infrastructure
- Public awareness regarding cyber safety is still limited
- India requires stronger proactive cybersecurity strategies
